A letter for you

One Reason against A Thousand Reasons Why

Two truths held at once.
Both real. Both mine.

The Letter

I keep thinking about this in two parts.

One reason why I shouldn't stay.
And a thousand reasons why I want to.

And somehow, both are real at the same time.

◆

The one reason is simple, but it hurts in a way I can't explain properly. It's not that I don't care about you — it's the opposite. It's that I care too much, and I don't know how to hold you and your pain without losing myself a little every time you break.

I hear you when you talk about what's inside your head. I try to stay steady, to be someone you can lean on, to tell you it'll be okay. And for a moment, I believe it too. But after the conversation ends, it doesn't leave me the same. I carry it. I replay it. I worry in silence.

And I think maybe that's the part I can't ignore anymore.

Not because you are a burden — you're not.

But because I'm starting to realize I'm not strong enough to be both your comfort and still stay whole.

◆

And then there's the other side.

A thousand reasons why I want to stay.

Because when you're okay, even for a little while, you're someone I don't want to lose from my life. Because I see the parts of you that aren't your pain — the way you think, the way you feel deeply, the way you try even when everything feels heavy. Those parts matter to me more than I can put into words.

If things were different… if your mind wasn't fighting you like this… I think I would've stayed without even questioning it.

That's the hardest part.

Not choosing between loving you or not loving you.

But realizing that love alone isn't always enough to make something safe for both people.

◆

You letting me go because you care about my feelings — I understand it. But it still hurts more than I expected. Because it feels like losing you while still caring about you at the same time.

I don't hate you for any of this. I don't blame you either.

I just wish things were lighter for you. I wish your mind didn't turn against you the way it does. And I wish I knew how to help you without feeling like I'm slowly disappearing in the process.

Maybe that's the truth I didn't want to admit — that sometimes caring deeply still isn't enough to fix what's broken.

◆

But even with that, I hope you don't think you're alone in it. Even if I can't be what holds all of this for you, I still want you to be okay. Not because of me. Because you deserve to be here, even when your thoughts tell you otherwise.

And for me…

I think I have to learn how to love someone without drowning in it.

Even if it means letting go.
Even if it hurts every time I think about "what could've been."

There is more to say
